☀️ How to Treat Heat Stroke Safely

Heat stroke is a severe heat-related illness that occurs when the body overheats, often due to prolonged exposure to high temperatures.

Signs and Symptoms

  • High body temperature (above 103°F or 39.4°C)
  • Altered mental state or confusion
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Rapid heartbeat

First Aid Steps

  1. Call emergency services immediately.
  2. Move the person to a cooler place.
  3. Remove excess clothing.
  4. Cool the person with water or ice packs.
  5. Monitor their temperature until help arrives.

When to Seek Medical Help

  • If the person is unconscious or unresponsive.
  • If symptoms worsen despite cooling efforts.
