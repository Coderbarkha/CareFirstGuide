Puncture wounds are deep, narrow wounds caused by sharp objects. They can be serious and may require medical attention.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Bleeding
  • Swelling
  • Redness
  • Pain
  • Possible infection signs (pus, fever)

First Aid Steps

  1. Wash your hands thoroughly.
  2. Apply direct pressure to stop bleeding.
  3. Clean the wound with soap and water.
  4. Apply an antibiotic ointment.
  5. Cover the wound with a sterile bandage.
  6. If the object is still embedded, do not remove it; seek medical help.

When to Seek Medical Help

  • If the wound is deep or won't stop bleeding.
  • If you notice signs of infection (redness, swelling, pus).
  • If the object is still embedded in the wound.
