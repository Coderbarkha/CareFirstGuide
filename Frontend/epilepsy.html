Epilepsy is a neurological disorder characterized by recurrent seizures. Seizures can vary in severity and duration.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Loss Of conciousness
  • Muscle Jerking
  • Staring spells
  • Confusion
  • Unusual sensations

First Aid Steps

  1. Stay calm and ensure the person's safety.
  2. Move any nearby objects that could cause injury.
  3. Place a soft object under their head to prevent injury.
  4. Time the seizure; if it lasts more than 5 minutes, call emergency services.
  5. Do not put anything in their mouth or restrain them.
  6. After the seizure, help them into a comfortable position and monitor their breathing.
  7. Stay with them until they are fully alert and aware.

When to Seek Medical Help

  • If the seizure lasts more than 5 minutes.
  • If another seizure follows immediately.
  • If the person is injured during the seizure.
  • If the person does not regain consciousness after the seizure.
