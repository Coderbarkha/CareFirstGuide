Bleeding can occur from cuts, scrapes, or injuries. It can be external (visible) or internal (not visible).

Signs and Symptoms

  • Visible blood loss
  • Pain at the injury site
  • Weakness or dizziness

First Aid Steps

  1. Apply direct pressure to the wound with a clean cloth.
  2. If bleeding does not stop, add more cloths without removing the first one.
  3. Elevate the injured area above the heart if possible.
  4. If bleeding is severe, seek emergency medical help immediately.

When to Seek Medical Help

  • If bleeding does not stop after 10 minutes of applying pressure.
  • If there are signs of infection (redness, swelling).
  • If the injury is deep or large.
