Asthma is a chronic condition that affects the airways, making it difficult to breathe. It can be triggered by allergens, exercise, or stress.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Shortness of breath
  • Coughing or wheezing
  • Tightness in the chest
  • Difficulty speaking or performing normal activities

First Aid Steps

  1. Stay calm and reassure the person.
  2. Help them sit up straight and loosen tight clothing.
  3. If they have a prescribed inhaler, assist them in using it.
  4. If symptoms do not improve, call for emergency help.

When to Seek Medical Help

  • If the person is unable to speak full sentences.
  • If their lips or face turn blue.
  • If they are not responding to their inhaler.

Prevention Tips

  • Avoid known triggers (allergens, smoke, etc.).
  • Use a peak flow meter to monitor lung function.
  • Follow an asthma action plan provided by a healthcare professional.
  • Keep rescue inhalers accessible at all times.
