A drug overdose occurs when a person takes more than the medically recommended amount of a drug, leading to harmful effects.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Confusion or altered mental state
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Unresponsiveness or unconsciousness
  • Seizures

First Aid Steps

  1. Call emergency services immediately.
  2. If the person is conscious, try to keep them calm.
  3. If they are unconscious, check their breathing and pulse.
  4. If necessary, perform CPR until help arrives.

When to Seek Medical Help

  • If the person is unresponsive or has difficulty breathing.
  • If they show signs of severe confusion or agitation.
  • If symptoms do not improve with first aid measures.

Prevention Tips

  • Always follow prescribed dosages for medications.
  • Avoid mixing drugs or alcohol without medical advice.
  • Keep medications out of reach of children.
