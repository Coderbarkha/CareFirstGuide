Ear bleeding can occur due to various reasons, including injury, infection, or foreign objects. It is important to handle it properly.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Bleeding from the ear
  • Pain or discomfort in the ear
  • Fluid drainage
  • Hearing loss

First Aid Steps

  1. Keep the person calm and still.
  2. Do not insert anything into the ear.
  3. If there is bleeding, apply a clean cloth to the ear.
  4. Do not tilt the head to the side; keep it upright.
  5. If bleeding continues or if there is severe pain, seek medical help immediately.

When to Seek Medical Help

  • If bleeding lasts more than 10 minutes.
  • If there is severe pain or swelling.
  • If there is a foreign object in the ear.

Prevention Tips

  • Avoid inserting objects into the ear.
  • Keep ears dry and clean.
  • Use ear protection when swimming or showering.
