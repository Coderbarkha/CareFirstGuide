Choking occurs when an object blocks the airway, preventing breathing. It can be life-threatening and requires immediate action.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Inability to talk or cough
  • Blue lips or skin
  • Weakness or loss of consciousness
  • High-pitched wheezing or coughing sounds

First Aid Steps

  1. Encourage the person to cough forcefully.
  2. If the person cannot cough, perform the Heimlich maneuver:
    • Stand behind the person and wrap your arms around their waist.
    • Make a fist with one hand and place it just above the person's navel.
    • Grab your fist with the other hand and thrust inward and upward.
    • Repeat until the object is expelled or the person becomes unconscious.
  3. If the person becomes unconscious, call emergency services and start CPR.

When to Seek Medical Help

  • If the object is not expelled after several attempts.
  • If the person loses consciousness.
  • If there are signs of severe respiratory distress.
