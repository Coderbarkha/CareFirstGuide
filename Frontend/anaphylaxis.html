Anaphylaxis is a severe allergic reaction that can be life-threatening. It requires immediate medical attention.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Difficulty breathing or wheezing
  • Swelling of the face, lips, or throat
  • Rapid or weak pulse
  • Skin rash or hives
  • Dizziness or fainting

First Aid Steps

  1. Call emergency services immediately.
  2. If the person has an epinephrine auto-injector, help them use it.
  3. Keep the person lying down and calm.
  4. If they are having difficulty breathing, loosen tight clothing.
  5. If symptoms do not improve, a second dose of epinephrine may be needed after 5-15 minutes.

When to Seek Medical Help

  • If the person has difficulty breathing or swelling of the throat.
  • If they lose consciousness or have a weak pulse.
  • If symptoms do not improve after using an epine phrine auto-injector.
  • If they have a history of severe allergies and experience any symptoms.

Prevention Tips

  • Avoid known allergens (foods, medications, insect stings).
  • Carry an epinephrine auto-injector if you have a history of severe allergies.
  • Wear a medical alert bracelet or necklace.
  • Inform friends and family about your allergies and how to use the auto-injector.
  • Have an emergency action plan in place.
