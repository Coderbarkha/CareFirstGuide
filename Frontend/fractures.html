A fracture is a break in the bone, which can be complete or partial. It can occur due to trauma, falls, or stress on the bone.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Severe pain at the injury site
  • Swelling or bruising
  • Deformity of the limb
  • Inability to move the affected area

First Aid Steps

  1. Call emergency services if you suspect a fracture.
  2. Keep the person still and calm.
  3. If possible, immobilize the injured area with a splint.
  4. Apply ice packs to reduce swelling.
  5. Do not attempt to realign the bone.

When to Seek Medical Help

  • If there is an open fracture (bone protruding through the skin).
  • If there are signs of shock (confusion, weakness).
  • If the pain is severe and does not improve with rest.
