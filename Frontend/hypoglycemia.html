Hypoglycemia, or low blood sugar, can occur in people with diabetes or those who have not eaten for a long time. It is important to recognize and treat it quickly.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Shakiness or trembling
  • Confusion or irritability
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Weakness or fatigue

First Aid Steps

  1. Give the person a quick source of sugar (e.g., glucose tablets, juice, candy).
  2. If they are unconscious, do not give them anything to eat or drink.
  3. Monitor their symptoms and provide additional sugar if needed.
  4. If symptoms do not improve within 15 minutes, seek medical help.

When to Seek Medical Help

  • If the person is unconscious or unresponsive.
  • If they have seizures or severe confusion.
  • If symptoms do not improve with first aid measures.

Prevention Tips

  • Eat regular meals and snacks.
  • Monitor blood sugar levels if diabetic.
  • Avoid excessive alcohol consumption.
