Hyperventilation is a condition where a person breathes very quickly, leading to a decrease in carbon dioxide levels in the blood.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Rapid breathing
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Tingling in the fingers or toes
  • Chest pain or tightness

First Aid Steps

  1. Encourage the person to breathe slowly and deeply.
  2. Have them breathe into a paper bag (if available) to increase carbon dioxide levels.
  3. Keep them calm and reassure them.
  4. If symptoms persist, seek medical help.

When to Seek Medical Help

  • If the person is unable to calm down.
  • If they show signs of confusion or fainting.
  • If symptoms do not improve with first aid measures.

Prevention Tips

  • Practice relaxation techniques such as deep breathing or meditation.
  • Avoid triggers that may cause anxiety or panic.
  • Stay hydrated and maintain a balanced diet.
  • Engage in regular physical activity to reduce stress.
