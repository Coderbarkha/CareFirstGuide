❄️ How to Handle Hypothermia Emergencies

Hypothermia occurs when the body loses heat faster than it can produce it, causing a dangerously low body temperature.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Shivering
  • Confusion or memory loss
  • Slurred speech
  • Weak pulse
  • Fatigue or drowsiness

First Aid Steps

  1. Move the person to a warm, dry place.
  2. Remove wet clothing and cover them with warm blankets.
  3. Provide warm, non-alcoholic beverages if conscious.
  4. If severe symptoms occur, seek emergency medical help immediately.

When to Seek Medical Help

  • If the person is unconscious or unresponsive.
  • If they show signs of severe hypothermia (confusion, weak pulse).
  • If symptoms do not improve with first aid measures.

Prevention Tips

  • Dress in layers and wear waterproof clothing.
  • Avoid prolonged exposure to cold weather.
  • Stay dry and avoid getting wet.
  • Be cautious when swimming in cold water.
