Nosebleeds are common and usually not serious. They can occur due to dry air, allergies, or injury.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Bleeding from one or both nostrils
  • Feeling of fullness in the nose
  • Swelling or redness around the nose
  • Possible headache or dizziness

First Aid Steps

  1. Stay calm and reassure the person.
  2. Have them sit up straight and lean slightly forward.
  3. Pinch the soft part of the nose for 10 minutes.
  4. Apply a cold compress to the back of the neck.
  5. If bleeding continues after 20 minutes, seek medical help.

When to Seek Medical Help

  • If the bleeding lasts more than 20 minutes.
  • If the person has difficulty breathing.
  • If the nosebleed is due to a head injury.
  • If there is a lot of blood or if the person feels faint.

Additional Information

For more information on nosebleeds, you can visit the Mayo Clinic.
